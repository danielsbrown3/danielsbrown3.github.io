How the World Works: A Scientists Guide to Our Past, Present and Future by Vaclav Smil

Introduction

The more specialized we become as a society the more information we accumalte, the greater burden younger generations face in terms of acquiring larger and larger sums of knowledge. If information has accumalated for such long periods of time, then why isn't everyone in society an expert? Smil argues that in society we operate with black boxes whose mechanisms require simple inputs and spit out complex outputs. When someone operates an iPhone they don't need the knowledge of the underlying physics principles that allow their phones to operate.
